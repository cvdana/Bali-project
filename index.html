WHY BALI?

Bali, the Land of the Gods

The Bali tradition adopted the pre-existing animistic traditions of the indigenous people. This influence strengthened the belief that the gods and goddesses are present in all things. Every element of nature, therefore, possesses its power, which reflects the power of the gods. A rock, tree, dagger, or woven cloth is a potential home for spirits whose energy can be directed for good or evil. Balinese Hinduism is deeply interwoven with art and ritual. And you will find intricately carved temples everywhere.

This place is a magical blend of culture, people, nature, activities, weather, culinary delights, nightlife, and beautiful accommodation. Moreover, Bali also enchants with its dramatic dances and colorful ceremonies, as well as its arts, and crafts.

ESSENTIALS

Experiences that define Bali

Pasih Uug Beach

Broken Beach is a scenic coastal formation on the southwestern edge of Nusa Penida. The spot is marked by a hilly rock arch, which is the star landmark of the area. Also known by its local name, Pasih Uug, it's a great spot for travel photographers and anyone who wants to enjoy stunning panoramic views.

Address

Broken Beach - Sakti, Nusa Penida, Kabupaten Klungkung, Bali 80771

Dance Performance

Dances are merged with stories and history in Bali. Many focus on the Sanskrit epics of India which are literature that convey cultural beliefs and structure. The two most well known are the Mahabharata and the Ramayana.

Address

Jl. Monkey Forest No.15, Ubud, Kecamatan Ubud, Kabupaten Gianyar, Bali 80571

Nyambu Village

Nyambu Village -declared Ecological Tourism Village by the Regent of Tabanan, Ni Putu Eka Wiryastuti in 2016 - offers tour package options that include tours of rice fields, local history, and religious tours. There are 67 temples that are designed beautifully and traditionally by the predecessors in the days of Ancient Bali.

Address

Nyambu Ecological Village, Kediri, Tabanan Regency, Bali 80771
